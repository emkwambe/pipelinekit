# **PIPELINEKIT-Design Partner & Beta Program Handbook**

## **Version 1.0**

# **Executive Purpose**

The purpose of the Design Partner Program is not to acquire customers.

The purpose is to learn.

PipelineKit succeeds or fails based on whether we discover:

1. What customers actually need.  
2. What customers will pay for.  
3. Which blueprints create the most value.  
4. What prevents trust in analytics.  
5. What operational pain is worth solving.

The first goal is learning.

Revenue is secondary.

# **Definition of a Design Partner**

A Design Partner is not merely a beta user.

A Design Partner:

* meets regularly  
* provides feedback  
* shares operational challenges  
* helps prioritize roadmap decisions  
* agrees to evaluate PipelineKit in a real environment

In return:

* discounted pricing  
* direct founder access  
* priority support  
* influence over product direction

### **Red Flag 2**

# **Founder Operating Principles**

Listen more than you explain.

Measure behavior, not compliments.

Prioritize repeated pain points.

Do not build features requested by only one customer.

Protect simplicity.

Stay focused on trusted analytics.

---

# **Beta Program Success Definition**

The Beta Program succeeds when:

1. Five organizations deploy PipelineKit.  
2. Three organizations become paying customers.  
3. Three case studies are published.  
4. Product positioning is validated.  
5. A repeatable onboarding process exists.  
6. Time-to-Trusted-Data is consistently less than one day.

At that point, PipelineKit transitions from an idea into a repeatable business.
