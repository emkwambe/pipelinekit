# **Purpose**

This document explains why PipelineKit pricing exists in its current form.

Future pricing changes must reference this document before implementation.

The objective is to preserve pricing rationale and prevent arbitrary changes.

---

# **Core Pricing Philosophy**

Customers do not purchase:

* Pipelines  
* Connectors  
* Compute  
* API Calls  
* Rows Processed

Customers purchase:

Trusted Analytics Outcomes

Pricing should align with business value delivered rather than technical activity generated.

---

# **Pricing Principles**

## **Principle 1**

Predictability Beats Optimization

Customers should understand their costs before deployment.

Unexpected bills reduce trust.

PipelineKit prioritizes predictable pricing over theoretical revenue maximization.

---

## **Principle 2**

Avoid Consumption Pricing

PipelineKit rejects pricing based on:

* Rows processed  
* Events processed  
* Pipeline executions  
* Data volume  
* Compute consumption

These models create uncertainty.

They also penalize successful customers.

---

## **Principle 3**

Value Increases With Organizational Adoption

The value of PipelineKit grows as more teams rely on trusted analytics.

Pricing should therefore scale with organizational usage rather than infrastructure activity.

---

## **Principle 4**

Customer Infrastructure Is Customer Cost

PipelineKit follows a BYO Infrastructure philosophy.

Customers pay directly for:

* Cloud resources  
* Warehouses  
* Storage  
* AI models

PipelineKit charges for orchestration, intelligence, and support.

---

# **Pricing Model Evaluation**

## **Option A**

### **Consumption-Based Pricing**

Examples:

* Fivetran  
* Snowflake  
* Datadog

Advantages:

* High revenue expansion

Disadvantages:

* Customer anxiety  
* Difficult forecasting  
* Billing disputes  
* Vendor lock-in perception

Decision:

Rejected

---

## **Option B**

### **Per-Pipeline Pricing**

Advantages:

* Easy to understand

Disadvantages:

* Discourages adoption  
* Encourages consolidation  
* Artificial limits

Decision:

Rejected

---

## **Option C**

### **Per-Connector Pricing**

Advantages:

* Simple packaging

Disadvantages:

* Penalizes integrations  
* Misaligned with customer value

Decision:

Rejected

---

## 

## **Option D**

### **Subscription Pricing**

Advantages:

* Predictable  
* Budget friendly  
* Easy forecasting  
* Aligns with outcomes

Decision:

Accepted

# **Revenue Philosophy**

PipelineKit should generate revenue from:

* Expertise  
* Blueprints  
* Diagnostics  
* Governance  
* Operational intelligence

PipelineKit should not generate revenue from:

* Customer data volume  
* Customer growth  
* Customer success

Success should reduce friction rather than increase cost.

---

# **Future Experiments**

All pricing changes require:

1. Hypothesis  
2. Success metric  
3. Expected outcome  
4. Actual outcome  
5. Final decision

No pricing change should occur without documented evidence.
